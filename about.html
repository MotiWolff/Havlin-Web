From Kemenitz to Lubavitch

Rabbi Shlomo Zalman - The Beginning of it All

Born in 1877, Russia

So that's how it all started with great-grandfather Shlomo Zalman. He was born in 1877 in Russia (imagine life then!). He was a follower of the Lubavitcher Rebbe, and in 1911 he immigrated to Israel on the Rebbe's mission. It was not an easy journey in those days.

The Family Accomplishments

Grandparents did and do a lot of cool things:

  1. They founded the Menachem Temple Institute in Jerusalem in 1988. I remember as a child I would walk around there and marvel at the amount of books.
  2. Grandma helps Grandpa run the "Jerusalem Rose" seminar for daughters of Chabad missionaries. It's always interesting to meet girls from all kinds of places in the world there and hear their stories.
  3. They built the synagogue 770 in Ramat Shlomo. I have a lot of good memories from there, especially from the prayers on Saturdays and holidays.

Grandpa also writes and edits a lot of books. Sometimes I help him arrange them in his office, and it always ends in an interesting conversation about some topic.
